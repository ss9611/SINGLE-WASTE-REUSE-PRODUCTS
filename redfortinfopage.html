Red Fort


The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila in Hindi, is a historic fort located in the heart of Old Delhi, India. The fort was constructed by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in the mid-17th century and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.


The Red Fort is known for its stunning red sandstone walls, which are nearly 33 meters tall and surround an area of more than 250 acres. The fort is home to a number of beautiful buildings and structures, including the Diwan-i-Am, which was used by the Mughal emperors for public audiences, and the Diwan-i-Khas, which was used for private audiences.


The Red Fort is also known for its stunning sound and light show, which takes place every evening and provides a fascinating glimpse into the history and culture of the Mughal Empire.
