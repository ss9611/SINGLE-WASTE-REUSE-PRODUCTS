Elephanta Caves


Elephanta Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site located on Elephanta Island, which is about 10 kilometers from Mumbai (Bombay), in the state of Maharashtra, India. The caves are known for their stunning rock-cut sculptures and architecture, which date back to the 5th and 6th centuries.


The caves consist of two groups of rock-cut temples, known as the Eastern and Western groups. The main attraction is the 7-meter tall statue of Lord Shiva in the Mahesha-Murti, or Trimurti, form. The statue depicts the three faces of Lord Shiva - the creator, the preserver, and the destroyer.


The caves are accessible by a short ferry ride from Mumbai's Gateway of India. Visitors can explore the various structures within the caves, including the impressive carvings of Hindu deities, the beautifully crafted pillars, and the intricate reliefs and sculptures.
