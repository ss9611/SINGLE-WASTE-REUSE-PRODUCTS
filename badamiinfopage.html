Badami


Badami is a small town located in the Bagalkot district of the state of Karnataka in India. It is situated at the base of a red sandstone cliff that towers over the town, and is famous for its ancient rock-cut temples and beautiful architecture. The town is located about 500 km from Bangalore, the state capital.


The most famous attraction in Badami is the Badami Cave Temples, a complex of four rock-cut temples that were built during the Chalukya dynasty between the 6th and 8th centuries. These temples are carved into the sandstone cliffs and are dedicated to Hindu deities such as Lord Shiva and Lord Vishnu. The intricate carvings, sculptures, and architectural details on the temple walls are stunning and are considered to be some of the finest examples of Indian rock-cut architecture.


In addition to its historical and cultural attractions, Badami is also known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities. The town is surrounded by hills and valleys, making it an ideal destination for trekking, rock climbing, and hiking. The nearby Banashankari Temple and Pattadakal, both UNESCO World Heritage Sites, are also worth a visit for their stunning architecture and historical significance.
