Thanjavur


Thanjavur, also known as Tanjore, is a historic city located in the Indian state of Tamil Nadu. It is situated on the banks of the River Cauvery, about 350 km south of the state capital Chennai, and is known for its rich cultural and historical heritage.


The city is most famous for its magnificent Brihadeeswarar Temple, which was built by the Chola dynasty in the 11th century and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and features stunning Dravidian-style architecture, intricate carvings, and beautiful frescoes. It is also known for its massive granite dome, which weighs over 80 tonnes and was hoisted into place without the use of modern technology.


Another popular attraction in Thanjavur is the Thanjavur Palace, which was built by the Nayak dynasty in the 16th century. The palace features a mix of architectural styles, including Dravidian, Nayak, and Maratha, and houses a museum that displays a collection of bronze sculptures, paintings, and other artifacts.
