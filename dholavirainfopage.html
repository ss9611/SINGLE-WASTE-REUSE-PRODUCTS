Dholavira


Dholavira is an ancient city located in the Indian state of Gujarat, dating back to the Indus Valley Civilization, also known as the Harappan civilization. It is one of the five largest Harappan sites in the Indian subcontinent and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.


Dholavira is known for its impressive urban planning and architecture, including the remains of a large fortified citadel, a sophisticated water management system, and several public buildings, including a marketplace, a large hall for public gatherings, and a series of reservoirs.


One of the most impressive features of Dholavira is its sophisticated water management system, which included a series of reservoirs, dams, and canals to collect and distribute water throughout the city. The city's well-planned layout and advanced engineering techniques suggest a highly organized and developed civilization.


Visitors to Dholavira can explore the ruins of the ancient city, including the remains of the citadel, the public buildings, and the water management system. The site also features a small museum where visitors can see some of the artifacts found at the site and learn more about the history and culture of the Indus Valley Civilization.
